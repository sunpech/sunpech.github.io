End of section 1. Point: Twitter List UI/UX* sucks.

Onto section 2....

Soon after my tweet, a twitter follower pointed me to Zach Holman's blog post, Don't Give Your Users Shit Work. I agree with many of the points Zach points out, that social web software shouldn't make it a pain to maintain circles/lists/groups-- the shit work. The software needs to be smart in making default common groups, and being able to decide where friends/contacts go-- smart work.

I disagree in the following situations:

  1. When there isn't enough good meta info of a contact to place in a proper lists.
  2. When the list isn't for private use. It's for public use.
The first point, when there isn't great meta info for the software, the list needs further human curation. The software may not be able to decide the best list to place the contact.

For example, you've connected with someone on Facebook that's not connected to your network, school, work, or any group. You are close friends, but there are no initial indications that says otherwise. You would need to manually add them to your "Close Friends" group.

The second point is the most important, when the lists being maintained isn't even for your personal use, but rather for public consumption. The lists that I've generated I do not use whatsoever. They're purely for others to view and follow if they feel that it would be of interest to them. 

For example: Chicago entities. I selected user-groups/meetups/entities in Chicago that I like and put them in a list. Twitter doesn't know that is the purpose of the list I just created. It's not a list I personally use since all my tweets are in a single column in Tweetdeck. The list is for someone who possibly thinks I know something or that I have good taste, and would like to follow what I think are good Chicago tweeple.

My point is that it's not shit work nor smart work. It's just work that needs human thought and time to accomplish. Maybe my work is all for nothing, that no one finds it useful. I can live with that.

* = UI is User Interface. UX is User Experience.
