As much as I love my Canon EOS 60D, I find that there are times when I don't want to lug around a DSLR around, and would prefer a simple point and shoot camera.

I really wanted the Canon PowerShot S100, but they've been sold out for quite some time and I couldn't wait for reorders. To me, the big selling point of the S100 is the built in GPS. I think all cameras, sooner rather than later, should have such a feature built right in.


So I thought I would compare some photos taken with my Canon Powershot S95 with the 60D (with Canon EF 16-35mm f/2.8L II USM Ultra Wide Angle Zoom Lens and a simple UV filter). Note: on my 60D, I shot in P mode with auto-ISO, and on my S95, auto.

Overall, I'm very happy with the quality of the S95. The colors are vibrant and sharp in the well lit pictures. Although I would say that compared with my 60D, it doesn't quite pop some images out as well. It's still exactly what I want, a small point and shoot that performs well for the times when I want to leave my DSLR at home.
